    • Does Level 2 Charging Harm the Battery?

    The battery of your electric vehicle is perhaps the single most important component that determines its longevity. Much like your cell phone or laptop, EV’s rely on lithium-ion batteries to operate which have low self-discharge, high energy efficiency, high power-to-weight ratio, and a long life. However, the most important element that affects lithium-ion batteries is charging.

    Since the advent of electric vehicles, various studies have been carried out around the effects of different levels of charging on an EV’s longevity, especially the effects of level 2 charging. Today, we’ve taken out the time to discuss these findings and pull you out of this skepticism around level 2 charging and its effects on an electric vehicle's battery.



    What is level 2 EV Charging?

    Level 2 EV charging is the most common type of charging option used by EV owners. Similar to electrical outlets used for other larger home appliances like ovens and dryers, a level 2 charger uses a 240-volt power source to ensure a more efficient charging process. With power requirements ranging from 3.3 kW to as high as 22 kW, level 2 chargers can charge an EV much faster than level 1 chargers.

    Level 2 chargers are typically found in homes, workplaces, and charging stations. They can be hardwired into an electrical system or plugged in using a NEMA 14-50 outlet. Many level 2 chargers come with smart features like RFID cards, scheduling charge times, and load balancing. Level 2 charging is a prime choice for EV owners who use their EVs frequently and have longer commutes.



    Benefits of Level 2 EV Charging

    Using a level 2 EV charger comes with numerous benefits which makes it an attractive option for EV owners. Not only are they much faster than standard level 1 chargers, they’re highly efficient in terms of energy usage and offer convenience for when and where you’d like to charge your EV. Here are some common benefits of EV level 2 chargers:



    1. Fast Charging

    Level 2 chargers are significantly faster than standard level 1 chargers, capable of providing a full charge to your EV in a fraction of the time it takes for level 1 chargers. This ensures that your EV isn’t always plugged in when it’s not in use. Depending on the power output of your level 2 charger and your EV’s hardware, level 2 charging can charge for 0% to 100% in 4 to 8 hours.



    2. Cost Saving and Charging Efficiency

    Level 2 chargers are known for their high levels of efficiency and cost effectiveness. Besides charging your EV in a few hours and ensuring that your EV is not always plugged in, level 2 chargers also come with various smart features like scheduling charge times, allowing you to charge during off-peak hours and take advantage of lower electricity rates. This also contributes to a more sustainable use of energy where less charging time is required which means less use of energy.



    3. Convenience

    Level 2 chargers can be hardwired or integrated into home energy management systems. Having a level 2 charger installed in your home means never having to visit public charging stations. It’s a unique experience only EV owners can have and was never possible with internal combustion engine vehicles. Level 2 chargers are rapidly being installed in public charging stations as well, which means EV owners can use level 2 charging at various locations making it a highly convenient option.

    Potential Concerns with Level 2 Charging

    While there are numerous benefits attached to level 2 charging, the potential concerns and drawbacks cannot be ignored. Here are some common risk attached to level 2 charging that EV owners must be aware of:

    1. Heat Generation

    One of the major concerns that level 2 charging carries is heat generation. Level 2 charging is a much faster charging option than level 1 charging, and faster charging delivers more energy which increases the risk of heating an EV’s battery. Frequent exposure to high temperature accelerates the degradation of lithium ion batteries and negatively affects the performance and longevity of an electric vehicle. Heat generation is also caused by overcharging, which happens when an EV’s battery continues to receive electrical energy beyond its full capacity.

    However, most level 2 chargers are well equipped with advanced monitoring systems, withholding the flow of energy once the EV is fully charged. Moreover, newer EV models come with thermal management systems that mitigates the effects of heat, even with faster charging. This is done by actively cooling the battery during the charging process which significantly reduces the risk of heat- induced damage.

    2. Battery Degradation

    Battery degradation usually occurs due to factors like heat generation, frequency of charging, and depth of discharge. The risk attached to level 2 chargers isn’t just about heat generation due to faster charging, it’s also about the increased frequency of using level 2 charging. Both these factors are major contributors of accelerated battery degradation which reduces the battery’s capacity, negatively impacting the EV’s range and longevity.

    To minimize the risk of battery degradation from level 2 charging, EV owners can adopt strategies like avoiding frequent deep discharges, keeping the battery at an ideal operating temperature, and occasionally using alternative charging methods such as level 1 or public fast chargers, rather than relying solely on level 2 charging.

    2. Minimizing the Risk of Level 2 Charging

    The risk attached to level 2 charging can be minimized by adhering to best practices when charging your EV. These will significantly reduce the chances of battery degradation and positively affect the performance and longevity of your EV.

    1. Comply with Manufacturer Guidelines

    Complying with guidelines provided by your level 2 charger’s manufacturer is crucial. Ensuring that the charging durations, voltage levels, and charging methods that you apply while charging are aligned with manufacturer’s guidelines ensures a minimized risk exposure to your EV’s battery.

    2. Ensure Professional Installation

    Installing a level 2 charging station, especially if you’re planning to hardwire it to your home’s electrical system, necessitates the services of a professional electrician. Lack of professional service or a DIY approach not only increases the risk of safety hazards and non-compliance with your area’s electrical code, it also affects the efficiency of your level 2 charger, which in turn negatively affects your EV’s battery. A qualified electrician will ensure compliance with electrical code, prevent safety hazards, and ensure that your level 2 charger operates effectively.

    3. Avoid Frequent Fast Charging

    Frequent use of fast chargers whether it’s level 2 charging or DC fast charging is not recommended due to the risk of heat generation. Make sure to give your EV a couple of days or more before you charge it again. If you must, and if time is not an issue, it’s better to use a level 1 charger. A combination of using level 1, 2 and DC fast charging and charging to a maximum of 80% rather than 100% is the best way to avoid any risk of battery degradation.

    Conclusion

    Level 2 charging doesn’t inherently harm your EV battery, but frequent use and heat generation can accelerate battery degradation over time. To protect your battery’s longevity, follow manufacturer guidelines, avoid frequent fast charging, and consider mixing in slower charging options like Level 1 when possible.

    Modern EVs come equipped with thermal management systems to minimize risks, but it’s still important to be mindful of your charging habits. Professional installation of your charger and charging to 80% instead of 100% can further reduce wear.
